TREMARI

Where three seas meet

1

Build Strategic Stock

Identify your top 3-5 bestselling pieces (by analyzing Etsy sales data monthly). Pre-produce 15-20 units of each before peak seasons (November-December, February for Valentine's, May for Mother's Day). Store in labeled containers with production date.

2

Document Everything

Create a production manual with: materials list with supplier links, step-by-step assembly photos, time per piece, quality control checklist. Use Notion or Google Docs. Update quarterly. This allows you to train help or step away without chaos.

3

Supplier Diversification

Never rely on one AliExpress supplier. Have 2-3 backup sources for chains, findings, and tags. Test quality from each. Keep a supplier scorecard (delivery time, quality, communication). Review every 6 months.

4

Launch Limited Collections

Instead of constant new products, launch seasonal drops: "Summer Solstice Collection" (June), "Winter Ritual Collection" (November). Limit to 50-100 pieces. Creates urgency + predictable production cycles you can plan around.

5

Hire Smart Help

When you hit 50+ orders/month consistently for 3 months, hire a part-time packing assistant (5-10 hours/week). Pay €12-15/hour. Train them on your packaging system using your documented process. Start with just packing, not production.

6

Shipping Automation

At 100+ orders/month, integrate Etsy with SendCloud or ShipStation (€20-40/month). Auto-generates labels, tracking updates customers, consolidates carriers. Saves 15-20 hours/month.

7

Pre-Pack Efficiency

Every Sunday, assembly-line your packaging: 20 pouches with tissue + sticker, 20 thank you cards with your signature pre-printed (just add names). Store in bins. Cut packing time from 10 min to 3 min per order.

8

Fulfillment Partners (Year 2+)

Once you're at 200+ orders/month and can't keep up, explore Italian 3PL services or EU fulfillment centers. They store inventory, pack, ship. You send bulk. Cost: €3-5 per order. Only do this when your margin supports it (€15+ profit per piece).

9

Strategic Product Expansion

Month 6-9: Add "Gold Collection" (18K plated, €35-45 price point). Month 12: Launch earrings to match bestselling necklaces. Month 18: Custom engraving service (+€15). Don't add more than 3-5 new SKUs per quarter—focus beats variety.

10

Own Your Platform

Month 6: Secure tremari.com domain (€68/year). Month 9-12: Launch Shopify site (€29/month). Keep Etsy for discovery, use website for repeat customers + higher margins (no Etsy fees). Drive traffic via email + Instagram.

11

Wholesale & Partnerships

Month 12+: Approach 5-10 Italian boutiques or concept stores in Milan, Rome, Florence. Offer consignment first (they pay after sale), then wholesale (50% off retail). Minimum order: 10 pieces. Creates brand credibility + steady income.

12

Gifting & Subscriptions

Year 2: Launch "TREMARI Gift Box" (curated set, premium packaging, €75-95). Test quarterly jewelry subscription for loyal customers (€40/month, exclusive piece). Builds recurring revenue + VIP community.

13

Content That Converts

Post 4x/week: Behind-the-scenes (Monday), styling tips (Wednesday), customer spotlight (Friday), Three Seas philosophy (Sunday). Film in batches once/month. Use CapCut for quick edits. Hire UGC creator for €50-100/month at Month 6.

14

Email Marketing Foundation

Month 1: Start collecting emails via Linktree. Month 3: Launch Mailchimp free tier. Send monthly: new drops, styling guides, customer stories. Month 6: Segment list (buyers vs browsers). Goal: 20% open rate, 3% click rate.

15

Micro-Influencer Strategy

Target: 5K-50K followers in jewelry, minimalist fashion, Italian lifestyle. Offer: Free piece (€25 value) for Instagram story + feed post. Send 5 packages/month. Track with unique discount codes. Best ROI: nano-influencers (1K-10K).

16

Paid Ads (When Ready)

Don't start ads until Month 6 and €1K profit saved. Test Etsy Ads first (€5/day, 30 days). If ROAS >3x, scale to €10/day. Month 12: Test Meta Ads (Instagram/Facebook, €10/day) targeting Italian women 25-40 interested in jewelry, minimalism, Mediterranean lifestyle.

17

Virtual Assistant

Month 9: Hire Filipino VA on Upwork (€5-8/hour, 10 hours/week). Tasks: Responding to Etsy messages, updating listings, scheduling social posts, order tracking. Saves you 10 hours/week for €200-320/month.

18

Financial Systems

Month 1: Use Google Sheets to track revenue, COGS, profit per product. Month 6: Switch to QuickBooks (€15/month) or Freeagent. Hire bookkeeper quarterly (€100-150) to review taxes, VAT, compliance. Never mix personal + business money.

19

Seasonal Planning

Every January: Map out year—product launches (Q2, Q4), content themes, holiday prep. Block 2-week production sprints before peak seasons. Schedule 1-week breaks after busy periods. Prevents burnout, creates predictable workflow.

20

Reinvestment Strategy

Split profits: 50% back into business (inventory, tools, marketing), 30% savings/emergency fund, 20% personal pay. First €5K profit: Upgrade photography setup (camera, lighting, €500-800). Next €5K: Professional brand photos (€800-1,200).

Supply Chain & Production

Risks:

  • AliExpress delays (20-45 days shipping to Italy)
  • Material quality inconsistency between batches
  • Out-of-stock on bestselling components during peak season
  • Damage/loss during international shipping
  • Unable to fulfill orders due to illness or emergency

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Maintain 2-3 vetted suppliers per component type with order history
  • Order materials 60 days before peak seasons (October for holiday rush)
  • Keep 3-month safety stock of critical items (tags, bestselling chains)
  • Request photos before shipment for bulk orders over €100
  • Document full production process so trained helper can step in (see Scaling #2)
  • Consider AliExpress Premium Shipping (+€10-20, arrives 10-15 days) for urgent restocks

Cash Flow & Profitability

Risks:

  • Revenue drops 40-60% in slow months (January, August in Italy)
  • Etsy fee increases (currently 6.5% transaction + 3-4% payment processing)
  • Supplier price hikes (metal, shipping costs)
  • Unexpected refunds eating into profit
  • Currency fluctuations (EUR/USD for some suppliers)

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Build 6-month operating expense reserve (target: €2,000-3,000 by Month 12)
  • Review profit margins monthly—if below 60%, raise prices or cut costs
  • Diversify income: 70% Etsy, 20% website, 10% wholesale by Year 2
  • Set clear refund policy (14 days, unworn, buyer pays return shipping)
  • Track actual profit per SKU—cut products below 50% margin
  • Hedge seasonal dips: Launch Valentine's collection (Feb), Mother's Day (May), Holiday (Nov-Dec)

Regulatory & IP Protection

Risks:

  • Operating without proper Italian business registration/VAT
  • EU jewelry regulations (nickel content <0.05%, marking requirements)
  • Trademark infringement (using protected terms/designs)
  • Someone copying TREMARI designs or brand name
  • Customer claims of allergic reactions

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Register as "Ditta Individuale" or SRL within first 3 months of consistent sales
  • Obtain VAT number when revenue exceeds €65,000/year (or earlier if selling to EU businesses)
  • Source only nickel-free, hypoallergenic stainless steel—request compliance certificates from suppliers
  • File EU trademark for TREMARI (€850-1,000) once revenue hits €10K (Month 6-9)
  • Watermark all product photos with subtle TREMARI logo
  • Clearly state "hypoallergenic stainless steel" in all listings + include care card disclaimer
  • Get liability insurance once revenue exceeds €20K/year (€200-400/year)

Shipping & Fulfillment

Risks:

  • Lost packages (Poste Italiane 1-2% loss rate)
  • Customs delays/fees for non-EU customers
  • Negative reviews from shipping delays outside your control
  • Packaging arrives damaged
  • Customer expects faster shipping than stated

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Always use tracked shipping for orders >€30 (Poste Italiane Posta 1 or BRT)
  • For non-EU: clearly state customs disclaimer in listing + message at purchase
  • Respond to shipping questions within 2 hours during business hours
  • Use rigid mailers (€0.30-0.50 each) for jewelry—bubble mailers not enough
  • Set realistic delivery windows: Italy 3-5 days, EU 7-12 days, Rest of World 15-30 days
  • Proactively message buyers when package is delayed >5 days with tracking update
  • Keep "problem order" log in Google Sheets—track carrier, issue, resolution time

Customer Perception & Competition

Risks:

  • 1-star review from unrealistic customer expectations
  • Copycat sellers on Etsy/AliExpress using similar designs
  • Inconsistent brand visuals across platforms
  • Social media backlash or negative comments
  • Losing brand differentiation as you scale

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Respond to ALL reviews within 24 hours—thank positive, offer solution for negative
  • For bad reviews: publicly apologize + offer replacement, privately request review update after resolution
  • Create brand guidelines document (fonts, colors, tone)—use for all posts, listings, packaging
  • Subtle branding: TREMARI engraved on tags, embossed on packaging—hard to copy
  • Register social handles early: @tremari.jewelry across Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, Facebook
  • Monitor "TREMARI jewelry" search monthly on Google, Etsy, Instagram—report copycats to platforms
  • Never engage with trolls publicly—respond once professionally, then ignore or block

Founder Dependency & Burnout

Risks:

  • Illness or personal emergency halts all operations
  • Burnout from 60-80 hour weeks during growth phase
  • Over-dependence on Etsy algorithm (can change overnight)
  • No backup for critical tasks (customer service, order fulfillment)
  • Losing passion for the brand

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Create "Emergency Operations" document: supplier contacts, Etsy login, email templates, where inventory is stored. Share with trusted person.
  • Set "vacation mode" boundaries: 1 week off every quarter, auto-responder active, pause orders or build 2-week buffer stock
  • By Month 9, diversify platforms: 60% Etsy, 30% own website, 10% wholesale—never 100% reliant on one channel
  • Automate repetitive tasks by Month 6: email autoresponder, social scheduling (Buffer/Later), Etsy message templates
  • Join entrepreneur community (online or local)—share challenges, get support
  • Every 3 months, ask: "Am I still excited about this?" If no for 2 quarters in a row, pivot the product line or business model

Quarterly Risk Review Process

  1. Every 3 months (January, April, July, October):
  2. • Review this entire risk document—update what's changed
  3. • Check your "problem order" log—identify patterns (same supplier? same carrier?)
  4. • Test your emergency plan—can someone access your systems if needed?
  5. • Review financials: Is your emergency fund growing? Margins healthy?
  6. • Ask: "What almost went wrong this quarter?" Then fix it.
  7. Keep a "Risk Diary": Note every small problem (delayed shipment, unhappy customer, supplier issue). Review patterns monthly. Prevention > reaction.
